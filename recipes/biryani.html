Biryani Recipe

Description:

Biryani is a popular South Asian mixed rice dish that is typically made with spices, vegetables, meat, and/or seafood. There are many variations of biryani, depending on the region, and each has its own unique blend of flavors and ingredients.

Here's a recipe for chicken biryani, which is one of the most popular types of biryani:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of basmati rice
  • 1 lb of boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of ghee or vegetable oil
  • 2-inch piece of ginger, peeled and grated

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice several times until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in cold water for at least 30 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with the yogurt, half of the chopped onion, half of the minced garlic, half of the grated ginger, and half of the chopped cilantro and mint. Season with 1/2 teaspoon of salt and 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper. Let the chicken marinate for at least 30 minutes or overnight.
  3. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the cinnamon sticks, cardamom pods, cloves, and cumin seeds and stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add the remaining chopped onion and saut√© for 5 minutes until translucent.
  5. Add the remaining minced garlic and grated ginger and stir for 1 minute until fragrant.
  6. Add the coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, and 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper and stir for 30 seconds until the spices are well combined.
  7. Add the marinated chicken and cook for 5-7 minutes until the chicken is browned on all sides.
  8. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot. Stir to combine with the chicken and spices.
  9. Add 2 cups of water and bring the mixture to a boil. Lower the heat to a simmer, cover the pot, and cook for 15-20 minutes until the rice is cooked and the water has been absorbed.
  10. Remove the pot from the heat and let it sit for 10 minutes. Add the remaining chopped cilantro and mint and the lemon juice and stir gently.
  11. Serve the biryani hot with raita (a yogurt-based side dish) and/or chutney. Enjoy!
